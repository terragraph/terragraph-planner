Interference Minimization

After site selection is complete and finalized, the next step selects the links and sectors in order to minimize interference in the network. At this stage, P2MP, deployment, and interference constraints are incorporated into optimization.

Note: the exact implementation of the objective function and/or constraints in the code might slightly differ from what is written here, but they should be equivalent.

Objective Function​

The objective is to minimize the shortage in the network just as in Coverage Maximization.

min⁡∑i∈SDEMϕi\min\sum_{i \in \mathcal{S}_{DEM}} \phi_imini∈SDEM​∑​ϕi​

or

max⁡min⁡i∈SDEM(di−ϕi)\max\min_{i \in \mathcal{S}_{DEM}} \left (d_i - \phi_i \right)maxi∈SDEM​min​(di​−ϕi​)

However, we also want as many additional links to be included as possible in order to ensure as much redundancy in the network as possible. Otherwise, the optimizer has no incentive to include links that do not serve to minimize the shortage. Thus, we also include the term

∑(i,j)∈Lwi,jℓi,j\sum_{(i,j) \in \mathcal{L}} w_{i,j} \ell_{i, j}(i,j)∈L∑​wi,j​ℓi,j​

where the weight wi,jw_{i,j}wi,j​ is a decreasing function of the link's length. Thus, the total objective function becomes

min⁡(M∑i∈SDEMϕi−∑(i,j)∈Lwi,jℓi,j)\min \left(M \sum_{i \in \mathcal{S}_{DEM}} \phi_i - \sum_{(i,j) \in \mathcal{L}} w_{i,j} \ell_{i, j} \right)min⎝⎛​Mi∈SDEM​∑​ϕi​−(i,j)∈L∑​wi,j​ℓi,j​⎠⎞​

or

max⁡(Mmin⁡i∈SDEM(di−ϕi)+∑(i,j)∈Lwi,jℓi,j)\max \left(M \min_{i \in \mathcal{S}_{DEM}} \left (d_i - \phi_i \right) + \sum_{(i,j) \in \mathcal{L}} w_{i,j} \ell_{i, j} \right)max⎝⎛​Mi∈SDEM​min​(di​−ϕi​)+(i,j)∈L∑​wi,j​ℓi,j​⎠⎞​

where MMM is some large value (i.e., some relative weighting between the two components of the objective function).

Constraints​

Most of the constraints are similar to those before (Flow Balance and Flow Capacity are unchanged) but with some slight modifications due to the addition of the link selection decision variable ℓi,j\ell_{i,j}ℓi,j​.

In addition to the Time Division Multiplexing constraint from before, time division multiplexing on a link is forced to 0 if the link is not selected.

Due to Flow Capacity, this automatically ensures that

Polarity​

The polarity of connected sites must be opposite.

ℓi,j≤pi+pj          ∀(i,j)∈L:i,j∈SPOP∪SDN\ell_{i,j} \leq p_i + p_j\; \; \; \; \; \forall (i,j) \in \mathcal{L}:i,j \in \mathcal{S}_{POP}\cup\mathcal{S}_{DN}ℓi,j​≤pi​+pj​∀(i,j)∈L:i,j∈SPOP​∪SDN​
ℓi,j≤2−pi−pj          ∀(i,j)∈L:i,j∈SPOP∪SDN\ell_{i,j} \leq 2 - p_i - p_j\; \; \; \; \; \forall (i,j) \in \mathcal{L}:i,j \in \mathcal{S}_{POP}\cup\mathcal{S}_{DN}ℓi,j​≤2−pi​−pj​∀(i,j)∈L:i,j∈SPOP​∪SDN​

This ensures that if ℓi,j>0\ell_{i,j} > 0ℓi,j​>0, then pi≠pjp_i \neq p_jpi​=pj​.

Sector​

σi,k≤si          ∀k∈Ki,i∈S\sigma_{i,k} \leq s_i\; \; \; \; \; \forall k \in \mathcal{K_i},i \in \mathcal{S}σi,k​≤si​∀k∈Ki​,i∈S

In this case, sis_isi​ is no longer a decision variable but simply a value based on the site selection decisions of the previous optimization steps.

σi,k=σi,l          ∀k,l∈Ki:nk=nl,i∈S\sigma_{i, k} = \sigma_{i, l}\; \; \; \; \; \forall k,l \in \mathcal{K_i}:n_k=n_l,i \in \mathcal{S}σi,k​=σi,l​∀k,l∈Ki​:nk​=nl​,i∈S

A link can only be selected if the sectors it is connected to are also selected.

Symmetric Link​

Backhaul links are bi-directional but are modeled with two separate directed links. If a link is selected, its reverse must also be selected.

ℓi,j=ℓj,i          ∀(i,j)∈L:(j,i)∈L\ell_{i, j} = \ell_{j, i}\; \; \; \; \; \forall (i, j) \in \mathcal{L}: (j, i) \in \mathcal{L}ℓi,j​=ℓj,i​∀(i,j)∈L:(j,i)∈L

Point-to-Multipoint​

A DN sector can connect to a limited number of other sectors. The number of DN-DN connections, PD\mathcal{P}_{D}PD​, and the number of total connections, PT\mathcal{P}_{T}PT​, are each limited (both are user-specified configurations).

∑j∈SPOP∪SDN:(i,j)∈Λi,kℓi,j≤PD          ∀k∈Ki,∀i∈S\sum_{j \in \mathcal{S}_{POP}\cup\mathcal{S}_{DN}:(i, j) \in \Lambda_{i, k}} \ell_{i, j} \leq \mathcal{P}_{D}\; \; \; \; \; \forall k \in \mathcal{K}_{i},\forall i \in \mathcal{S}j∈SPOP​∪SDN​:(i,j)∈Λi,k​∑​ℓi,j​≤PD​∀k∈Ki​,∀i∈S

Generally, PD=2\mathcal{P}_{D} = 2PD​=2 and PT=15\mathcal{P}_{T} = 15PT​=15.

CN Link​

CNs can only have a single incoming link

∑i∈S:(i,j)∈Lℓi,j≤1          ∀j∈SCN\sum_{i \in \mathcal{S}:(i,j) \in \mathcal{L}} \ell_{i, j} \leq 1\; \; \; \; \; \forall j \in \mathcal{S}_{CN}i∈S:(i,j)∈L∑​ℓi,j​≤1∀j∈SCN​

Deployment Guidelines​

We enforce two deployment guidelines/constraints: for any two links leaving different sectors on the same site,

  • The angle between them must be at least α\alphaα
  • The angle between them must be at least θ\thetaθ if the ratio of their link distances (larger distance over smaller) is greater than ρ\rhoρ.

Typical values are α=25\alpha = 25α=25, θ=45\theta = 45θ=45 and ρ=3\rho = 3ρ=3 (all are user-specified configurations).

We identify all link pairs (i,j)∈L(i, j) \in \mathcal{L}(i,j)∈L and (i,k)∈L{(i, k) \in \mathcal{L}}(i,k)∈L that violate these conditions. Define Q\mathcal{Q}Q to be the set of all such {(i,j),(i,k)}\{(i, j),(i,k)\}{(i,j),(i,k)} link pairs.

ℓi,j+ℓi,k≤1          ∀{(i,j),(i,k)}∈Q\ell_{i, j} + \ell_{i, k} \leq 1\; \; \; \; \; \forall \{(i, j),(i, k)\} \in \mathcal{Q}ℓi,j​+ℓi,k​≤1∀{(i,j),(i,k)}∈Q

Interference​

In order to incorporate interference into the model, we use the SINR of a link to determine its MCS class which is then used to modify its capacity. The SINR is determined based on how much time interference-causing links are transmitting and how much interference they cause while the interfered-on link is simultaneously transmitting. All of this is expressed using a linear function of various decisions variables.

The SINR (in dBm) on link (i,j)(i,j)(i,j) is calculated as

SINRi,j=10log⁡10RSLi,jNp+∑(k,l)τk,lIk,li,jSINR_{i,j} = 10\log_{10}\frac{RSL_{i,j}}{N_p + \sum_{(k,l)}{\tau_{k,l}I^{i,j}_{k,l}}}SINRi,j​=10log10​Np​+∑(k,l)​τk,l​Ik,li,j​RSLi,j​​

where RSLi,jRSL_{i,j}RSLi,j​ is the received signal level for the link (i,j)(i, j)(i,j) in mW, Ik,li,jI^{i,j}_{k,l}Ik,li,j​ is the interference caused by the link (k,l)(k,l)(k,l) on the link (i,j)(i, j)(i,j) in mW, and NpN_pNp​ is the noise power in mW. In order for the link (k,l)(k, l)(k,l) to cause interference on link (i,j)(i, j)(i,j):

  • There must be LOS from site kkk to site jjj
  • The receiving sector for link (i,j)(i, j)(i,j) must be the same as the receiving sector for the LOS (k,j)(k, j)(k,j)
  • The transmitting sector for link (k,l)(k, l)(k,l) must be the same as the transmitting sector for the LOS (k,j)(k, j)(k,j)
  • Sites jjj and kkk must have opposite polarity.

Time division multiplexing is incorporated into this equation in order to scale the amount of interference based on how much time the interference-causing link is actually transmitting. While not precise, for planning purposes, this hopefully provides a reasonable approximation. For example, it is useful for removing redundant links from interference considerations.

Ik,li,jI^{i,j}_{k,l}Ik,li,j​ is pre-computed prior to the optimization. An issue with this is that through transmit power modulation, the links do not necessarily transmit at full power. However, incorporating this as a decision variable would greatly complicate this model. Thus, for planning purposes, the worst-case-scenario of maximum transmit power is assumed.

Unfortunately, as written, the expression for SINR is not linear in the decision variables. However, define Si,j=10SINRi,j10S_{i,j}=10^\frac{SINR_{i,j}}{10}Si,j​=1010SINRi,j​​ (converting SINR from dBm to mW), then

Multi-Channel Constraints​

For networks that can support it, enabling multi-channel can reduce the amount of interference and improve overall network performance. The assumption is that links on different channels do not interfere with each other. Thus if link (k,l)(k, l)(k,l) is causing a lot of interference on link (i,j)(i, j)(i,j), the optimizer can put those links on different channels to remove the interference.

Critically, we do not currently model link capacity differences between the channels. We use a single driving frequency and derive the link capacities and interference from that. A future improvement would be to address this shortcoming. However, because of this, the channel selected by the optimizer has an arbitrary association with the actual channel number. Some post-processing might involve assigning the most commonly selected channel to the one with the best real-world performance.

There are two constraints that are impacted by multi-channel planning. The first is Deployment Guidelines and the second is Interference. That is, links on different channels do not violate deployment guidelines and do not cause interference on each other.

Formally, the channel decision is associated with each sector. However, instead of having several channel decisions for each sector, the sector decisions themselves are slightly modified. Whereas before we had σi,k\sigma_{i,k}σi,k​ decision variables, we now have σi,k,c\sigma_{i,k,c}σi,k,c​ for c∈{1,…,C}c \in \{1,\ldots,C\}c∈{1,…,C}. That is, there are now CCC sector decisions for each physical sector, with each one corresponding to a sector on a particular channel. This is not the case for CN sectors which automatically take on the channel of the serving DN and therefore do not need additional decision variables.

A link can only be selected if the sectors it is connected to are also selected.

ℓi,j≤∑c=1Cσi,k,c          ∀(i,j)∈L:(i,j)∈Λi,k\ell_{i, j} \leq \sum^{C}_{c=1} \sigma_{i,k,c}\; \; \; \; \; \forall (i, j) \in \mathcal{L}: (i, j) \in \Lambda_{i, k}ℓi,j​≤c=1∑C​σi,k,c​∀(i,j)∈L:(i,j)∈Λi,k​
ℓi,j≤∑c=1Cσj,κ,c          ∀(i,j)∈L:(i,j)∈Λj,κ\ell_{i, j} \leq \sum^{C}_{c=1} \sigma_{j,\kappa,c}\; \; \; \; \; \forall (i, j) \in \mathcal{L}: (i, j) \in \Lambda_{j, \kappa}ℓi,j​≤c=1∑C​σj,κ,c​∀(i,j)∈L:(i,j)∈Λj,κ​

For multi-channel, an additional constraint requires that the two sectors connecting the link must have the same channel. This additional constraint is not required if the receiving site is a CN.

ℓi,j≤σi,k,c−σj,κ,c+1          ∀(i,j)∈L:(i,j)∈Λi,k∩Λj,κ,∀c∈{1,…,C}\ell_{i,j} \leq \sigma_{i,k,c} - \sigma_{j,\kappa,c} + 1\; \; \; \; \; \forall (i, j) \in \mathcal{L}: (i, j) \in \Lambda_{i, k}\cap \Lambda_{j, \kappa}, \forall c \in \{1,\ldots,C\}ℓi,j​≤σi,k,c​−σj,κ,c​+1∀(i,j)∈L:(i,j)∈Λi,k​∩Λj,κ​,∀c∈{1,…,C}
ℓi,j≤σj,κ,c−σi,k,c+1          ∀(i,j)∈L:(i,j)∈Λi,k∩Λj,κ,∀c∈{1,…,C}\ell_{i,j} \leq \sigma_{j,\kappa,c} - \sigma_{i,k,c} + 1\; \; \; \; \; \forall (i, j) \in \mathcal{L}: (i, j) \in \Lambda_{i, k}\cap \Lambda_{j, \kappa}, \forall c \in \{1,\ldots,C\}ℓi,j​≤σj,κ,c​−σi,k,c​+1∀(i,j)∈L:(i,j)∈Λi,k​∩Λj,κ​,∀c∈{1,…,C}

Thus, if σi,k\sigma_{i,k}σi,k​ and σj,κ\sigma_{j,\kappa}σj,κ​ are on the same channel, then these constraints become ℓi,j≤1\ell_{i,j} \leq 1ℓi,j​≤1. If they are on different channels, then for some channel, the one of the constraints becomes ℓi,j≤0\ell_{i,j} \leq 0ℓi,j​≤0.

Multi-Channel Deployment Guidelines​

The deployment guidelines/constraints only apply if the two sectors that the links are leaving from are on the same channel. If they are on different channels, then the constraint does not apply. To model this, we introduce binary decision variables, we will call them deployment links, ζi,j,c∈{0,1}\zeta_{i,j,c} \in \{0,1\}ζi,j,c​∈{0,1} for all the links in Q‾\mathcal{\overline{Q}}Q​ which is 1 if link ℓi,j\ell_{i,j}ℓi,j​ is on channel ccc. Q‾\mathcal{\overline{Q}}Q​ is the "flattened" version of Q\mathcal{Q}Q containing each link of all link pairs separately and it should be much smaller than L\mathcal{L}L, so we do not need to do this for all links.

A deployment link can only be selected if its corresponding link is selected.

ζi,j,c≤ℓi,j          ∀(i,j)∈Q‾,∀c∈{1,…,C}\zeta_{i,j,c} \leq \ell_{i,j}\; \; \; \; \; \forall (i, j) \in \mathcal{\overline{Q}}, \forall c \in \{1,\ldots,C\}ζi,j,c​≤ℓi,j​∀(i,j)∈Q​,∀c∈{1,…,C}

Furthermore, the deployment link is connected to a sector of the same channel.

ζi,j,c≤σi,k,c          ∀(i,j)∈Q‾:(i,j)∈Λi,k,∀c∈{1,…,C}\zeta_{i,j,c} \leq \sigma_{i,k,c}\; \; \; \; \; \forall (i, j) \in \mathcal{\overline{Q}}: (i, j) \in \Lambda_{i, k}, \forall c \in \{1,\ldots,C\}ζi,j,c​≤σi,k,c​∀(i,j)∈Q​:(i,j)∈Λi,k​,∀c∈{1,…,C}
ζi,j,c≤σj,κ,c          ∀(i,j)∈Q‾:(i,j)∈Λj,κ,∀c∈{1,…,C}\zeta_{i,j,c} \leq \sigma_{j,\kappa,c}\; \; \; \; \; \forall (i, j) \in \mathcal{\overline{Q}}: (i, j) \in \Lambda_{j, \kappa}, \forall c \in \{1,\ldots,C\}ζi,j,c​≤σj,κ,c​∀(i,j)∈Q​:(i,j)∈Λj,κ​,∀c∈{1,…,C}

If the receiving site is a CN, the last constraint is slightly modified because there are no channel decisions on CNs.

ζi,j,c≤σj,κ          ∀(i,j)∈Q‾:(i,j)∈Λj,κ,∀c∈{1,…,C}\zeta_{i,j,c} \leq \sigma_{j,\kappa}\; \; \; \; \; \forall (i, j) \in \mathcal{\overline{Q}}: (i, j) \in \Lambda_{j, \kappa}, \forall c \in \{1,\ldots,C\}ζi,j,c​≤σj,κ​∀(i,j)∈Q​:(i,j)∈Λj,κ​,∀c∈{1,…,C}

However, the deployment link must be selected if the link is selected and both sectors on a particular channel are selected.

ζi,j,c≥ℓi,j+σi,k,c+σj,κ,c−2          ∀(i,j)∈Q‾:(i,j)∈Λi,k∩Λj,κ,∀c∈{1,…,C}\zeta_{i,j,c} \geq \ell_{i,j} + \sigma_{i,k,c} + \sigma_{j,\kappa,c} - 2\; \; \; \; \; \forall (i, j) \in \mathcal{\overline{Q}}: (i, j) \in \Lambda_{i, k}\cap \Lambda_{j, \kappa}, \forall c \in \{1,\ldots,C\}ζi,j,c​≥ℓi,j​+σi,k,c​+σj,κ,c​−2∀(i,j)∈Q​:(i,j)∈Λi,k​∩Λj,κ​,∀c∈{1,…,C}

Finally, the deployment guidelines constraint becomes

ζi,j,c+ζi,k,c≤1          ∀{(i,j),(i,k)}∈Q,∀c∈{1,…,C}\zeta_{i,j,c} + \zeta_{i,k,c} \leq 1\; \; \; \; \; \forall \{(i, j),(i, k)\} \in \mathcal{Q}, \forall c \in \{1,\ldots,C\}ζi,j,c​+ζi,k,c​≤1∀{(i,j),(i,k)}∈Q,∀c∈{1,…,C}

Multi-Channel Time Division Multiplexing​

In order to model multi-channel interference, we need to modify the time division multiplexing decision variable to incorporate channels. Whereas before we had τi,j\tau_{i,j}τi,j​ decision variables, we now have τi,j,c\tau_{i,j,c}τi,j,c​ for c∈{1,…,C}c \in \{1,\ldots,C\}c∈{1,…,C}.

Before dissecting the interference constraints, we first see how it modifies some of the other time division multiplexing constraints.

The sum of the time division multiplexing decision on each sector does not change much.

∑j∈S:(i,j)∈Λi,kτi,j,c≤σi,k,c          ∀k∈Ki,i∈S,∀c∈{1,…,C}\sum_{j \in \mathcal{S}:(i,j) \in \mathcal{\Lambda}_{i, k}} \tau_{i,j,c} \leq \sigma_{i,k,c}\; \; \; \; \; \forall k \in \mathcal{K_i},i \in \mathcal{S}, \forall c \in \{1,\ldots,C\}j∈S:(i,j)∈Λi,k​∑​τi,j,c​≤σi,k,c​∀k∈Ki​,i∈S,∀c∈{1,…,C}
∑j∈S:(j,i)∈Λj,kτj,i,c≤σi,k,c          ∀k∈Ki,i∈S,∀c∈{1,…,C}\sum_{j \in \mathcal{S}:(j,i) \in \mathcal{\Lambda}_{j, k}} \tau_{j,i,c} \leq \sigma_{i,k,c}\; \; \; \; \; \forall k \in \mathcal{K_i},i \in \mathcal{S}, \forall c \in \{1,\ldots,C\}j∈S:(j,i)∈Λj,k​∑​τj,i,c​≤σi,k,c​∀k∈Ki​,i∈S,∀c∈{1,…,C}

This also ensures that the time division multiplexing decision is 0 on channels that are not selected.

Flow capacity has to be updated

fi,j≤∑c=1Cτi,j,cti,j          ∀(i,j)∈Lf_{i,j} \leq \sum^{C}_{c=1} \tau_{i,j,c} t_{i,j}\; \; \; \; \; \forall (i,j) \in \mathcal{L}fi,j​≤c=1∑C​τi,j,c​ti,j​∀(i,j)∈L

as does the constraint that time division multiplexing is 0 if the link is not selected

∑c=1Cτi,j,c≤ℓi,j          ∀(i,j)∈L\sum^{C}_{c=1} \tau_{i,j,c} \leq \ell_{i,j}\; \; \; \; \; \forall (i,j) \in \mathcal{L}c=1∑C​τi,j,c​≤ℓi,j​∀(i,j)∈L

Multi-Channel Interference​

Due to the change in the time division multiplexing decision variable, χi,k,l\chi_{i,k,l}χi,k,l​ becomes χi,k,l,c\chi_{i,k,l,c}χi,k,l,c​ for c∈{1,…,C}c \in \{1,\ldots,C\}c∈{1,…,C}. Then, we have

Si,j,c−1=Np+∑(k,l)χi,k,l,cIk,li,jRSLi,jS^{-1}_{i,j,c}=\frac{N_p + \sum_{(k,l)}{\chi_{i,k,l,c}I^{i,j}_{k,l}}}{RSL_{i,j}}Si,j,c−1​=RSLi,j​Np​+∑(k,l)​χi,k,l,c​Ik,li,j​​

Critically, this is done for each channel separately. Thus, if all links (k,l)(k, l)(k,l) are communicating on a different channel than (i,j)(i, j)(i,j), ∑(k,l)χi,k,l,cIk,li,j=0\sum_{(k,l)}{\chi_{i,k,l,c}I^{i,j}_{k,l}} = 0∑(k,l)​χi,k,l,c​Ik,li,j​=0 because the time division multiplexing decision variable will be 0.

The remaining MCS class decision variable, μi,j,m\mu_{i,j,m}μi,j,m​ becomes μi,j,c,m\mu_{i,j,c,m}μi,j,c,m​ for c∈{1,…,C}c \in \{1,\ldots,C\}c∈{1,…,C}. Then we have

∑m=1Mμi,j,c,m≤1          ∀(i,j)∈L,∀c∈{1,…,C}\sum^{\mathcal{M}}_{m=1} \mu_{i,j,c,m} \leq 1\; \; \; \; \; \forall (i,j) \in \mathcal{L}, \forall c \in \{1,\ldots,C\}m=1∑M​μi,j,c,m​≤1∀(i,j)∈L,∀c∈{1,…,C}

and

Si,j,c−1≤Mμi,j,c,1+∑m=2Mμi,j,c,mυm          ∀(i,j)∈L,∀c∈{1,…,C}S^{-1}_{i,j,c} \leq M \mu_{i,j,c,1} + \sum^{\mathcal{M}}_{m=2} \mu_{i,j,c,m} \upsilon_m\; \; \; \; \; \forall (i,j) \in \mathcal{L}, \forall c \in \{1,\ldots,C\}Si,j,c−1​≤Mμi,j,c,1​+m=2∑M​μi,j,c,m​υm​∀(i,j)∈L,∀c∈{1,…,C}

Additionally, we have to enforce that the chosen MCS class has positive capacity for only one of the channels.

∑c=1Cμi,j,c,1≥C−1          ∀(i,j)∈L\sum^{C}_{c=1} \mu_{i,j,c,1} \geq C - 1\; \; \; \; \; \forall (i,j) \in \mathcal{L}c=1∑C​μi,j,c,1​≥C−1∀(i,j)∈L

With this, the flow is bounded by the appropriate throughput

fi,j≤∑c=1C∑m=1Mμi,j,c,mϑm          ∀(i,j)∈Lf_{i,j} \leq \sum^{C}_{c=1} \sum^{\mathcal{M}}_{m=1} \mu_{i,j,c,m} \vartheta_m\; \; \; \; \; \forall (i,j) \in \mathcal{L}fi,j​≤c=1∑C​m=1∑M​μi,j,c,m​ϑm​∀(i,j)∈L

Like before, technically the bound on the flow should also be scaled by τi,j,c\tau_{i,j,c}τi,j,c​, but for the same reason, it is omitted here.

Here, we must also ensure that the time division multiplexing decision corresponds to the channel that has positive capacity. Without this, say channel 0 has positive capacity and channel 1 has zero capacity: the flow is bounded by the capacity of the positive capacity channel independent of the time division multiplexing (and channel) decision itself. Thus, the optimizer could choose sector/link channel 1 but still have positive flow across the link.

τi,j,c≤1−μi,j,c,1          ∀(i,j)∈L,∀c∈{1,…,C}\tau_{i,j,c} \leq 1 - \mu_{i,j,c,1}\; \; \; \; \; \forall (i,j) \in \mathcal{L}, \forall c \in \{1,\ldots,C\}τi,j,c​≤1−μi,j,c,1​∀(i,j)∈L,∀c∈{1,…,C}
